# The YHC Way Presentation - Slide Narratives

A presenter's guide explaining the meaning and purpose of each slide in The YHC Way management operating system presentation.

---

## SECTION 1: Orientation & Purpose (Slides 1-5)

### Slide 1: Section Header - Orientation & Purpose
I use this opening section header to set the stage for what's coming. It gives me a natural pause to prepare the audience for understanding the foundational "why" behind The YHC Way system.

### Slide 2: Title Slide - The YHC Way
Here I introduce The YHC Way as our "Management Operating System" with the tagline "Work unified. Work smarter. Lead with clarity." I want to establish the core promise right away: this isn't just software, it's a way of running the business that brings order to chaos and clarity to decision-making.

### Slide 3: What Is The YHC Way?
On this slide, I answer the fundamental question of what the system actually is. I position The YHC Way as a unified workspace built specifically for how we operate at Yoga Health Center - not a generic tool, but a custom-designed cockpit. My key message here: one source of truth for decisions, follow-through, and accountability. I use this to help the audience understand this is about operational excellence, not just having another app.

### Slide 4: Why This System Matters
This is my benefits slide where I make the business case. I outline five key outcomes: faster execution, cleaner communication, predictable growth, better decisions, and scalable management. I close with "This is not a tool. It's how we run the business" to reinforce that adoption isn't optional - it's fundamental to how we operate.

### Slide 5: What You Will Stop Doing / Start Doing
Here I break down behavior changes by role. I address the GM, owners, front desk leads, and teachers/coaches individually, making the change tangible. I use this to show that everyone's daily work will improve - less chasing, less reacting, more clarity and ownership.

---

## SECTION 2: Daily & Weekly Operating System (Slides 6-10)

### Slide 6: Section Header - Daily & Weekly Operating System
With this section, I introduce the rhythms and routines that make the system work. I emphasize that the operating system isn't just software - it's the habits and cadences that drive consistent execution.

### Slide 7: Management Daily Workflow
This is the heartbeat of the system - the "5-minute" daily workflow. I walk through five steps: Start with briefing, Capture inbox items, Decide on top 3 priorities, Block calendar time, Execute on projects. I use this slide to show that effective management doesn't require hours - just discipline around a simple daily loop.

### Slide 8: Weekly Operating Rhythm
Here I cover our 60-90 minute weekly rhythm: Scoreboard Review, Issues List, Rocks/Projects, To-Do Review, and Decisions. This is our management meeting structure - not a status update, but an accountability session. I use this to establish expectations for what our weekly meeting looks like.

### Slide 9: Rules of the Road
On this slide, I establish the "source of truth" for each tool: Slack for real-time coordination, Inbox for intake, Tasks for ownership, Projects for multi-step work, Calendar for commitments. I reinforce critical behaviors: if it has an owner and due date, it's a task. Meetings end with who/what/by when. Slack is not the archive.

### Slide 10: Response Time Norms
I set specific expectations here because clear expectations reduce anxiety and interruptions. I explain: Slack is immediate/1 hour, same-day items go to Tasks, 24-48 hours for non-urgent, and weekly updates for Projects. I use this to create shared expectations across the team.

## SECTION 3: Setup & Configuration (Slides 11-14)

### Slide 11: Section Header - Setup & Configuration
This section covers the practical "getting started" steps. I use this to transition from concepts to action.

### Slide 12: Connect Accounts
I walk through a step-by-step list of integrations to connect in order: Google Workspace, Slack, Brevo, Typeform, Perkville, and YHCTime. This gives new users a clear onboarding path. I emphasize that the order matters - we start with the most critical connections first.

### Slide 13: Configure Defaults
Beyond connecting accounts, I explain that users need to configure their preferences: task labels, project templates, notification settings, and mobile setup. I include the troubleshooting tip (re-auth, permissions, popups) to help address common friction points early.

### Slide 14: Common Issues + Quick Fixes
This is my troubleshooting reference slide for the most common problems: Gmail not connected, missing Slack channels, calendar duplicates, Zoom issues, mobile loading problems, Chrome extension issues, and pop-up blockers. Having this reference reduces support requests and empowers self-service.

## SECTION 4: Core Workspace (Slides 15-19)

### Slide 15: Section Header - Core Workspace
This section covers the primary tools users will interact with daily. I call these our "home base" features.

### Slide 16: Dashboard Overview
I present the Dashboard as the daily command center - it shows the unified feed, upcoming meetings, task summary, quick actions, and AI Daily Briefing. I tell the audience "Start here every morning" to establish the Dashboard as the natural entry point for the day.

### Slide 17: Unified Mailbox
Here I explain how the Inbox brings emails and Slack messages into a single feed. Users can read, reply, or convert items to tasks. I emphasize reduced context switching and fewer missed messages. This is where intake happens.

### Slide 18: Email Features
I do a deeper dive into email capabilities: read/reply without Gmail, compose new emails, AI drafting, and delete with sync to Gmail trash. I show that the system can replace the need to open Gmail separately.

### Slide 19: Slack
I explain how Slack appears in the system: followed channels, full threads, customizable channel selection, and integration with the Unified Mailbox. I use the phrase "Slack becomes signal, not noise" to capture the value of curated channel viewing.

## SECTION 5: Planning & Execution (Slides 20-25)

### Slide 20: Section Header - Planning & Execution
This section covers the tools for getting things done: Calendar, Tasks, and Projects.

### Slide 21: Calendar Power
I show how the unified calendar brings together Google, Apple, and Calendly with multiple views, Zoom join buttons, and color-coded sources. I position the calendar as a "strategic tool" - not just showing what's scheduled, but helping users plan effectively.

### Slide 22: Managing Tasks
I provide a how-to guide for tasks: create, set due dates and priorities, add subtasks, mark complete, and use AI prioritization. I emphasize that tasks represent ownership and accountability - the foundation of execution.

### Slide 23: Task Features
I cover detailed features: due dates, reminders, priorities, subtasks, assignments, recurring tasks, comments, and Asana sync. This helps users understand the full capability of our task system.

### Slide 24: Managing Projects
I walk through how-to for projects: create projects, add columns, add tasks, drag-and-drop, and import from Asana. I explain that projects are for multi-step initiatives - bigger than a single task.

### Slide 25: Project Features
I cover Kanban boards, custom columns, task details with comments, subtasks, and Asana import. I position projects as the home for major initiatives and ongoing work streams.

## SECTION 6: AI Productivity Suite (Slides 26-29)

### Slide 26: Section Header - AI Productivity Suite
This section showcases our AI-powered features that amplify productivity.

### Slide 27: Using the AI Assistant
I present four key AI capabilities: Daily Briefing (summary of your day), Smart Search (Cmd/Ctrl+K), Task extraction from emails/messages, and Email drafting. I tell the audience "AI becomes your management co-pilot" to capture the value.

### Slide 28: AI Features
I provide a complete list of AI features: Daily Briefing, Smart Search, Email drafting, Task extraction, Calendar optimization, Task prioritization, and AI Summaries. This slide serves as a reference for what AI can do.

### Slide 29: AI Summarize Tool
I walk through the summarization tool: paste transcripts/emails/notes, add context, get structured summaries with action items. I note it's perfect for processing meeting notes and long email threads quickly.

## SECTION 8: Growth Engine (Slides 32-38)

### Slide 32: Section Header - Growth Engine
This section focuses on our business growth tools - tracking acquisition, conversion, and retention.

### Slide 33: The Growth Funnel
I introduce our three-stage funnel: Acquisition (lead sources, referrals, corporate outreach), Conversion (intro to membership), and Retention (risk list, outreach, win-backs). This is the conceptual model for how membership grows predictably.

### Slide 34: Weekly Scoreboard
I present the 8-12 metrics that matter: active members, new members, reactivations, expired, terminated, net adds, intro offers sold, conversion rate, plus optional attendance and autopay metrics. I call the scoreboard "the GM's steering wheel" - the numbers that guide our decisions.

### Slide 35: Marketing Data Hub
I explain how we centralize marketing signals: Brevo email analytics, Typeform responses, and Perkville rewards. Having all marketing data in one place helps us identify what's working.

### Slide 36: Intro Offers Tracking
I cover dedicated tracking for intro offer performance: conversion rates, offer type comparison, and data export. This turns intro offers into a measurable funnel rather than just sales.

### Slide 37: Creating QR Codes
I show our tool for creating branded, trackable QR codes with custom colors. I mention these are useful for events, signage, and marketing campaigns.

### Slide 38: Email Builder
I walk through building marketing emails: choose templates, edit content, preview on mobile/desktop, and send via Brevo. I explain how this creates polished marketing emails without needing design skills.

I walk through Sections 1-5 in the first session, covering the "why" and daily/weekly rhythms. I follow up with Sections 6-8 for tool-specific training.

**For Leadership Reviews:**
I focus on Slides 4 (Why This Matters), 8 (Weekly Rhythm), and 34 (Scoreboard) to reinforce management accountability.

**For Troubleshooting Reference:**
I direct people to Slide 14 (Common Issues) and Slides 44-46 (Feature Requests, Bug Reports, Getting Help) as quick references.

**For Marketing Team:**
I prioritize Section 8 (Growth Engine) covering the funnel, scoreboard, and marketing tools.
